## Types of Oral narratives

1. **Myths:** These are traditional stories that explain the origin of a certain community, often involving supernatural beings and mysterious events.
2. **Legends:** These are oral narratives that tell the story of a hero or heroine who lived in a certain community.
	 - Examples: Warriors and Leaders.
3. **Trickster narratives:** These are stories that involve a character cheating/fooling the other.
4. **Explanatory narratives:** These are stories that explain why certain things are the way they are. Example: Why the turtle has a cracked shell.
5. **Monster narratives:** These are narratives that have monsters/ogres, often having supernatural features as characters.
6. **Dilemma narratives:** These are stories that end with the characters facing two or more difficult choices.
## Ways to make a performance of a narration more interesting/Story telling techniques

1. **Facial expressions:** These are used to show emotions. Examples: Seeming sad, angry and happy.
2. **Gestures:** These are hand and head movements used to express meaning. Examples: Nodding your head, clapping hands.
3. **Tonal variation:** Varying your voice to pass a message. Example: Making your voice high in one sentence, then going low in another. They can also pass emotions.
